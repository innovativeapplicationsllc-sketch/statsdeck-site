ESPN · Private league setup

Connect your private ESPN league

Private ESPN leagues need two session cookies before StatsDeck can read them. This one-tap tool grabs them for you — no copying long codes by hand. It takes about a minute.

What you'll do

You'll add a small "bookmarklet" — a bookmark that runs a tiny action instead of opening a page. When you tap it while signed in to ESPN, it copies your two credentials, ready to paste back into your StatsDeck chat. You only set it up once.

Why this is safe: the bookmarklet reads only your two ESPN cookies and copies them to your clipboard. It sends nothing anywhere on its own — you stay in control, and you decide when to paste them into StatsDeck.

Pick your device:

1

Copy the bookmarklet code

Tap Copy below. You'll paste this in a moment when you create the bookmark.

The StatsDeck ESPN bookmarklet

javascript:(function(){…})();
2

Add it as a bookmark in Safari

Open Safari and go to fantasy.espn.com, signed in to your league. Tap the Share icon, then Add Bookmark, and Save. (Any page works — we'll fix the address next.)

[ screenshot: Safari Share sheet → Add Bookmark ]
3

Edit the bookmark and paste the code

Tap the book icon → Edit → tap your new bookmark. Clear the web-address line and paste the code you copied in Step 1. Rename it something like Get ESPN creds and tap Done.

[ screenshot: editing the bookmark, pasting the code into the URL field ]
4

Run it on your league page

While on fantasy.espn.com and signed in, open your bookmarks and tap Get ESPN creds. A box appears with your credentials text already selected — tap Copy.

[ screenshot: the popup showing the ready-to-copy credentials text ]
5

Paste it back into StatsDeck

Return to your StatsDeck chat and paste. That's it — StatsDeck saves your credentials and connects your private league.

That's the whole setup

Once you paste your credentials into StatsDeck, it verifies them and connects your league. You won't need to do this again unless your ESPN session expires.

Keeping your credentials safe

Your SWID and espn_s2 cookies are session keys for your ESPN account. Treat them like a password: only paste them into your StatsDeck chat, and if you ever want to invalidate them, log out of ESPN and back in — that issues fresh cookies.

Cookies expire. ESPN rotates these periodically. If StatsDeck ever says it can't read your private league anymore, just run the bookmarklet again to grab a fresh pair.
